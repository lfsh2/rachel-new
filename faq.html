Is this therapy? What's the difference?
Therapy primarily addresses mental health diagnosis and past trauma within a clinical framework — and it is a deeply valuable and sometimes necessary thing. Coaching focuses on where you are now, where you want to go, and how to move forward. My approach integrates evidence-based emotional healing tools (inner child work, IFS, nervous system regulation, attachment theory) within a gospel-rooted, faith-based framework. I am not a licensed therapist. I always refer clients to therapy when that's what they need, and many of my clients work with both a therapist and me simultaneously — they serve different purposes beautifully.
Is this biblical? Is what you teach scriptural?
Yes — deeply so. Everything I teach is anchored in a grace-rooted, Father-heart-of-God understanding of scripture. I've done significant personal and academic study of the gospel, particularly around the themes of adoption, belovedness, and rest. The emotional healing tools I use (IFS, attachment work, nervous system regulation) are not in conflict with scripture — they're tools for understanding how we're made and where we've been hurt. I have found them, when held in the context of the gospel, to be profoundly clarifying about who God is and who we are to Him.
What if my husband doesn't know I'm doing this — or doesn't support it?
This is a question I receive more often than you might think, and I hold it with a lot of tenderness. Many women come to this work carrying the weight of whether they're allowed to take up space for their own healing. You are. Coaching is your private space, and I will never share anything you bring to our sessions. If you're navigating a complicated dynamic with your husband around this, we can hold that as part of the work itself — because that dynamic is often deeply connected to the performance patterns we're addressing. I want this to be a safe, private container for you.
How do I know if this work is right for me?
If you're exhausted in a way that sleep doesn't fix — if you love God but your faith feels hollow and obligatory — if you perform wellness and service for everyone around you and secretly feel like you're disappearing — this work was designed for you. The free discovery call is specifically built to answer this question honestly, together. You don't need to have it figured out before you reach out. That's what the call is for.
How long until I see real results?
Most women notice real, felt shifts within the first four to six sessions — a new awareness of their own patterns, a moment of genuine softness toward themselves, a conversation with their husband or children that goes differently than it ever has. Deep, sustained transformation — the kind that changes how you wake up in the morning and what you believe about God — typically unfolds over three to six months of consistent, honest work. This is not a quick fix. It is the real, root-level thing. And the real thing takes time.
Which program is right for me?
That's exactly what the discovery call is designed to answer. I don't want you guessing from a webpage. We'll talk through where you are, what you need, and what your season actually looks like — and I'll give you my honest recommendation. Sometimes the 1:1 container is what's needed. Sometimes the group is the better fit. Sometimes the free training is genuinely the right first step. I'll tell you the truth about what I think, and we'll decide together.
What if I'm not sure I can afford it?
Let's have that conversation on the call. There are multiple program options designed for different seasons and financial situations. I will never pressure you toward something that isn't right for where you are. I also never want cost alone to be the reason a woman doesn't get help she genuinely needs — so let's talk honestly about what's possible before you decide that door is closed.
What does a typical session actually look like?
Sessions are 60 minutes via Zoom, just the two of us. We usually start by checking in on what's been alive for you since we last met — what came up, what shifted, what felt hard. Then we go where the work needs to go. Sometimes that's a structured inner child or IFS exercise. Sometimes it's a slower, deeper conversation about God and who He is to you. I come prepared but I follow your lead. In between sessions, 1:1 clients have access to voice memo support so you're never sitting alone with something hard or significant.
What if I've tried coaching before and it didn't work?
That's one of the most honest questions anyone can bring, and I respect it. Most coaching stays at the behavioral surface — helping you set better goals, build better habits, manage your schedule. What I do goes beneath that: into the underlying beliefs, childhood wounds, and nervous system patterns that are driving the behavior in the first place. When we change what's happening at the root, everything built on top of it begins to shift. That's the meaningful difference. I can't promise you an outcome — but I can tell you this is genuinely different work than what you've likely encountered before.
Can I pause or change programs if life gets hard?
Yes. Life happens — health, family, grief, seasons that simply get heavy. This work is designed to be sustainable, not another thing you're white-knuckling through. If something comes up and you need to adjust the pace or container, we figure it out together with honesty and grace. The goal is freedom — and that includes freedom from the pressure to perform even in your healing.
