My Story

The Breaking Point
That Finally
Set Me Free.

"I sat on my pantry floor and cried for an hour while my children played just three feet away. That was the moment I finally admitted: performing had stopped working. Everything changed after that afternoon."

I was raised in the church — the pastor's daughter who learned before she could articulate it that love was something you earned. Good grades, worship team, first to serve and last to need anything. I perfected the performance. I genuinely, deeply loved Jesus. But by my early thirties — wife, mother of two, ministry-adjacent on every side — I was quietly breaking. Angry at my children at the wrong moments. Numb in my marriage in a way I couldn't explain. Sitting in church with tears on my face from the music and feeling absolutely nothing when I opened my Bible alone.

My Instagram looked beautiful. I was disappearing inside it. Then came the pandemic, a season of forced stillness, and a courageous therapist who asked me questions no one had ever asked before. And then — slowly, tenderly, in ways I could not have manufactured — a God who turned out to be far more gentle than everything I had been taught. I've spent the years since doing the deep work: IFS and inner child healing, nervous system restoration, attachment theory, and a total re-encounter with the grace-rooted gospel that actually sets you free. I didn't set out to become a coach. I set out to survive. And when I came out the other side, I found I couldn't keep any of it to myself.

— Rachel
