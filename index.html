My Story

I Was the Woman Who
Had It All Together —
and Was Disappearing.

I sat on my pantry floor and cried for an hour while my children played just three feet away. That was the moment I finally admitted the truth: all the performing had stopped working. That afternoon changed everything.

I grew up the pastor's daughter who learned early that love was something you earned through achievement. Good grades, worship team, never needing anything — I perfected the performance. I genuinely loved Jesus. But by my early thirties, married with two kids, I was breaking. Angry at the wrong moments. Numb in my marriage. Reading my Bible and feeling absolutely nothing. My life looked beautiful from the outside. Inside, I was slowly disappearing. It took a pandemic, a courageous therapist, and a God who turned out to be far more tender than I had ever been taught — to finally bring me home to myself. I've spent the years since doing the deep work: inner child healing, IFS, nervous system restoration, attachment theory, and a total re-encounter with the grace-rooted gospel. And now I can't keep it to myself.

— Rachel
